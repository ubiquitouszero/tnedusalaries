Tennessee Technological University — Department of Chemistry
Data Sources: TN Tech FY2023-24 Personnel Budget | CUPA-HR | AAUP | Peer Institution Data

Executive Summary

TN Tech Chemistry faculty salaries are significantly below market. When benchmarked against R2 doctoral universities nationally and Tennessee peer institutions, TN Tech Chemistry salaries lag by 10-20% depending on rank. Combined with internal inversions and compression, this creates both retention risk and equity concerns that warrant immediate administrative attention.

Data Sources & Methodology

Primary Sources

  • TN Tech Internal: October Budget Personnel FY2023-24 (official university document)
  • CUPA-HR: College and University Professional Association for Human Resources 2024 Faculty Survey
  • R2 Benchmarks: UnivStats.com compilation of 133 R2 doctoral universities (2024)
  • TN Peers: MTSU and ETSU salary data from UnivStats and institutional HR databases
  • AAUP: American Association of University Professors 2023-24 Faculty Compensation Survey

Peer Selection Criteria

  • Carnegie Classification: R2 Doctoral Universities (High Research Activity)
  • Control: Public institutions
  • Region: Tennessee Board of Regents / THEC peer institutions
  • Discipline: Chemistry (CIP Code 40.05xx) where available; all-discipline averages as proxy

Key Benchmark Values (2024)

  • R2 Professor: $124,806
  • R2 Assoc Prof: $98,274

Recommendations for Faculty Senate

Proposed Resolution Points:
  1. Request CUPA Data Access: Ensure TN Tech HR provides department-level access to CUPA Data-on-Demand for Chemistry (CIP 40.05)
  2. Establish Peer Group: Formally adopt MTSU, ETSU, and 3-5 comparable R2 publics as salary benchmarks
  3. Create Equity Pool: Propose dedicated funding separate from merit increases ($50-75K annually)
  4. Address Inversions First: Prioritize internal inversions (est. $15K) before market adjustments
  5. Annual Review: Institute annual salary equity review comparing to CUPA medians by rank
