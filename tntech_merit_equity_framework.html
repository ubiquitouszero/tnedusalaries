Tennessee Technological University — Department of Chemistry
Data Sources: TN Tech FY2023-24 Personnel Budget | CUPA-HR | AAUP | Peer Institutions | TN Tech Policy 206

Executive Summary

Merit and market equity are complementary, not competing, goals. This analysis demonstrates that TN Tech Chemistry faculty are significantly below market rates (10-25% depending on rank) AND proposes a merit-based framework for determining adjustment priority. The recommendation: address gross inversions immediately, then use a merit-weighted formula for market adjustments over 3-5 years.

Part 2: Merit-Based Evaluation Framework

Why Merit Matters: Administration will not approve "across the board" increases without performance accountability. A merit-weighted approach ensures high performers benefit most while maintaining the equity correction. This framework aligns with TN Tech Policy 206 and standard R2 practices.

Standard Academic Weighting (R2 Universities)

Teaching 40%
Research 40%
Service 20%

Standard for R2 tenure-track faculty per UC Boulder, Texas State, MTSU. Teaching-focused positions may use 60/20/20 or 70/10/20.

Part 3: Integrated Merit-Equity Adjustment Model

Key Principle: Separate equity corrections from merit increases. Equity addresses structural underpayment relative to market. Merit rewards performance above expectations. Both are necessary; neither replaces the other.

Proposed Adjustment Formula

Total Adjustment = Equity Adjustment × Merit Multiplier

Where:
• Equity Adjustment = (Market Benchmark × Target Percentile) - Current Salary
• Merit Multiplier = 0.75 + (Merit Score × 0.125) [Range: 0.875 to 1.25]

Example: Rating 4 = 1.25x, Rating 3 = 1.0x, Rating 2 = 0.875x

This formula ensures all eligible faculty (≥3.0 rating) receive equity adjustments, while exceptional performers (4.0) receive 25% more than the base equity correction. Faculty rated 2.0-2.9 receive 87.5% of the equity adjustment plus a development plan.

Recommended Phased Implementation

Phase Timeline Focus Est. Cost
Phase 1 Year 1 Fix rank inversions (Asst Prof earning more than Assoc Prof) $15,200
Phase 2 Year 1-2 Fix experience inversions (junior earning more than senior at same rank) $8,000

Part 5: Faculty Senate Resolution Framework

Recommended Resolution Points:
  1. Establish Chemistry Salary Equity Review Committee — 3 tenured faculty + Chair, annual review
  2. Request CUPA Data Access — Chemistry CIP 40.05, R2 public peer group, Southeast region
  3. Adopt Merit-Weighted Equity Model — Performance multiplier tied to annual reviews
  4. Create Dedicated Equity Pool — Separate from merit increases (~$20K/year for Chemistry)
  5. Phase 1 Priority — Immediate correction of documented inversions ($15K)
  6. Annual Benchmarking — Compare to CUPA medians each year with transparent methodology
  7. Appeals Process — Faculty may appeal merit ratings per existing TN Tech Policy 206

Administrative Framing

Retention Risk Language: Faculty replacement cost is 1.5-2× annual salary (search, startup, lost productivity). A $5,000 retention raise costs far less than a $150,000+ replacement. The Chemistry department has already experienced attrition concerns; proactive equity investment protects institutional investment in faculty.
